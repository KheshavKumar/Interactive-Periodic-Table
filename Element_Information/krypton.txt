Krypton is a noble gas which means it does not react with many other elements. It is clear and has no taste or smell.1 The atmosphere is only about one millionth part krypton.1 The name kypton comes from the Greek word kryptos meaning hidden. It is used in fluorescent lamps flashbulbs and as a wavelength standard. The metre used to be defined as 1650763.73 wavelengths in a vacuum of krypton gas.2 Krypton has very few compounds.
